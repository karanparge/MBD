    MSBB

    The Maharashtra State Biodiversity Board (MSBB) is an autonomous body under the Government of Maharashtra's Department of Environment and Climate Change. It is situated in the premises of the Maharashtra State Forest Department. As per the provision made in Section 22 of the Biological Diversity Act, 2002, the State Government of Maharashtra constituted the Maharashtra State Biodiversity Board in the year 2008 vide Notification No. WLP/1008/CR/147/F-1, dated: 02/01/2008.

    The Board works under the Chairmanship of Hon. Minister for Environment & Climate Change, with Dr. Eknath B. Shinde as the Member Secretary of the Board. The Board includes nine other actively involved Members, comprising five ex-officio members representing the concerned departments of the State Government.

    State Animal: Indian Giant Squirrel

    (Ratufa Indica)

    The Indian giant squirrel (Ratufa indica) is a large multi-coloured tree squirrel species. It exhibits 2-3 types of color patterns with shades of black, brown, and deep red. The body varies from deep red to brown with white patches on the belly as well as dirty white or cream-colored under-parts and forelimbs. The Indian giant squirrel has pink lips and nose. The eyes are colored in bright dark or light brown. The powerful and long tail is light brown with a creamy white tip.

    State Bird: Yellow-footed Green Pigeon

    (Treron Phoenicoptera)

    The Yellow-footed Green Pigeon (Treron phoenicoptera), also known as Yellow-legged Green Pigeon, is a common species of green pigeon found in the Indian Subcontinent. It is the state bird of Maharashtra. In Marathi it is called Hariyal. The species feeds on fruit, including many species of Ficus. They forage in flocks. In the early morning they are often seen sunning on the tops of emergent trees in dense forest areas.

    State Butterfly: Blue Mormon

    (Papilio Polymnestor)

    Indian Blue Mormon (Papilio polymnestor polymnestor), is the state butterfly of Maharashtra. It’s a large swallowtail butterfly, member of the ” Papilionidae” (BIRDWING) family. With a wingspan of 120-150 mm, it is the fourth largest butterfly of India. It is common and not thought to be threatened. It occurs throughout the year but more common in the monsoon and immediately after it. The butterfly is most common in heavy rainfall areas, such as evergreen forests. 

    State Fish: Silver Pomfret

    (Pampus Argenteus)

    Pampus argenteus, the silver pomfret or white pomfret  is a species of butterfish that lives in the Indo-West Pacific, spanning the coastal waters of the Middle East, Eastern Africa, South Asia, Southeast Asia, and East Asia. Silver pomfrets are usually silver/white in color, with few small scales. They can grow up to a range of 4–6 kg (8–13 lb). However, due to overfishing, specimens weighing less than 1 kg (2 lb) are more commonly seen.

    State Flower: Jarul

    (Lagerstroemia Speciosa)

    Jarul is a beautiful flowering plant native to India and parts of Asia. It is known for its distinctive white flowers and fragrant leaves. It is often used as a decorative plant in gardens and parks, as well as being a popular choice for street tree planting. The leaves, bark, and seeds of the plant are used in traditional medicine to treat a variety of ailments, including skin conditions, fever, and respiratory issues.

    State Tree: Mango

    (Mangifera Indica)

    Mango, (Mangifera indica), member of the cashew family (Anacardiaceae) and one of the most important and widely cultivated fruits of the tropical world. The mango tree is considered indigenous to southern Asia. Mangoes are a rich source of vitamins A, C, and D. The tree is evergreen, often reaching 15–18 metres (50–60 feet) in height and attaining great age. The simple leaves are lanceolate, up to 30 cm (12 inches) long.

    Biodiversity

    Biodiversity describes the richness and variety of life on earth. The term biodiversity was coined in 1985. It is important in natural as well as artificial ecosystems. It deals with nature’s variety, the biosphere. It refers to variabilities among plants, animals and microorganism species.To safeguard its biodiversity Maharashtra has 6 national parks, 48 wildlife sanctuaries, and 6 conservation reserves as protected area networks (as per the India State of Forest Report, 2019).
